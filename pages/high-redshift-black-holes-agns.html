Project 1 / The life of Little Red Dots

Where do LRDs come from, and where do they go?

JWST has revealed compact sources with blue UV emission, red optical continua, broad emission lines in some cases, and inferred black-hole-to-stellar mass ratios that exceed both local scaling relations and current simulation predictions. Their abrupt emergence and disappearance over z ~ 5-8 make their origin and evolution especially puzzling.

Initial condition for LRD

Halo assembly bias in the early Universe

Author list: Zihao Wang, Fangzhou Jiang*

Assembly bias provides a way to test whether competing LRD formation scenarios occupy distinct halo environments.

  • Characterizes early-Universe halo assembly bias in the Shin-Uchuu cosmological N-body simulation.
  • Tracks how formation time, concentration, and spin change clustering at fixed mass and how those signals evolve toward high redshift.
  • Applies the bias model to direct-collapse black holes, low-spin compact galaxies, SIDM core-collapse halos, and primordial black-hole-like initiation.
  • Finds that the DCBH scenario predicts the strongest large-scale bias and enhanced pair fractions, while PBH-like initiation is close to unbiased.

Nuclear transition of LRD

How Little Red Dots turn blue

Author list: Zihao Wang, Kohei Inayoshi*

This ongoing work asks whether LRDs and compact blue broad-line AGN are evolutionary stages of the same black-hole growth process, different viewing geometries, or both.

  • Develops a time-dependent model for a Kelvin-Helmholtz contracting gaseous envelope around a growing black hole.
  • Includes radiative and mechanical feedback from the accretion flow back onto the envelope structure.
  • Predicts continuum signatures through transitional phases.
  • Uses the contrast among LRDs, Little Blue Dots, and normal AGNs to constrain feedback prescriptions.

Project 2 / Tracking AGN accretion history in radiation-hydrodynamics simulations

How do SMBHs grow, flicker, and affect the surrounding Universe?

The growth of SMBHs is governed by their accretion histories. This project uses realistic cosmological simulations to connect AGN fueling, halo assembly, galaxy growth, and environmental observables, especially proximity zones during and after the Epoch of Reionization.

Developing

Proximity zones

Connecting AGN accretion histories to hydrogen and helium proximity zones

Author list: Zihao Wang, Xuejian Shen

AGN proximity zones probe both the ionization state of the intergalactic medium and the recent growth history of SMBHs, but their interpretation is shaped by AGN variability, finite lifetime, neighboring sources, and line-of-sight density fluctuations.

  • Uses the LUMINA simulation to study HI proximity zones at z ~ 6-7 and HeIII proximity zones at z ~ 3-4.
  • Connects measured proximity-zone sizes to instantaneous luminosity, accretion history, and SMBH lifetime.
  • Tests how long ionization fronts retain memory of previous AGN activity.
  • Quantifies how neighboring galaxies, AGN, large-scale environment, and density fluctuations affect measured sizes.

Assembly and variability

Connecting AGN accretion histories to halos, galaxies, and large-scale environment

Author list: Zihao Wang, Xuejian Shen

SMBH accretion is stochastic across many timescales. This project characterizes individual black-hole growth tracks and links AGN activity to halo assembly, galaxy growth, and the surrounding large-scale environment.

  • Quantifies variability, burstiness, duty cycles, and characteristic timescales using tools such as power spectral density analysis.
  • Studies how these properties depend on black-hole mass, Eddington ratio, host-galaxy properties, and redshift.
  • Examines the roles of halo growth, mergers, gas inflows, and starbursts in triggering accretion events.
  • Builds a statistical framework for interpreting observed variability and SMBH growth at high redshift.
